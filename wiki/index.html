Decorations

Decorations are aesthetic objects that can be placed in the main game area with Buildings and on the edge exclusive to Obstacle and Decoration placement. They have no active purpose in defense or production. Some are permanently available in the shop, others for limited times via shop, events, or challenges. All can be stashed and repurchased from the shop.

Unlike functional buildings, decorations can be placed on the grassy border of the map—something no defense or resource collector can do. This allows for creative framing of your village without using valuable defensive tiles.

Key Characteristics

  • Placement: Can be placed in the main area or the border (unlike buildings).
  • Stashing: Can be stored in the layout editor or using a Shovel of Obstacles.
  • Availability: Some are always in the shop, others are limited-time event rewards.

Types of Decorations

Permanent Shop Items: Flags, Flowers, and Statues available for Gold, Elixir, or Gems.
Seasonal Exclusives: Rewards from Monthly Challenges (Silver/Gold Pass).
Event Specials: Extremely rare items from one-time events (e.g., Anniversary statues).

Obstacles

Obstacles are trees, logs, rocks, and other foliage that are randomly placed in your village. You start with about 40 obstacles around your village. They are the only buildings that spawn naturally over time!

45 Max Spawns
8h Respawn Time
0-6 Gems per Removal

Removing obstacles requires a Builder and a small amount of Gold or Elixir. In return, you gain Experience and often Gems.

Summary

Obstacles are structures that obstruct the placement of a village's Buildings. While the player can still build villages without being hindered by obstacles (by building around them or choosing a relatively clear location), it is significantly harder to do so at higher levels. To free up space for buildings to be placed, the player must either remove the obstacles or relocate it elsewhere or stash it using a Shovel of Obstacles.

If a Shovel of Obstacles is used on an obstacle, that obstacle will be permanently movable like any other building. The obstacle can also be stashed in Village Edit Mode similar to Decorations. The obstacle can also be removed normally afterwards, though this does not provide further compensation beyond the standard rewards for removal.

If you accidentally set a Villager to remove an obstacle, you can cancel it if the button is tapped before the Villager is finished. Canceling the removal of an obstacle will refund you 100% of the resources spent, compared to the 50% refund on all other upgrades.

Your village can have a maximum of 45 obstacles at one time - if this limit is reached or exceeded, regular obstacles will no longer spawn until obstacles are removed. Gem Boxes and holiday-themed obstacles ignore this limit when spawning and can spawn even if more than 45 obstacles are present on the village. Obstacles stashed in Village Edit Mode will still count towards this limit.

There is an achievement called "Nice and Tidy" which gives players Experience and Gems for removing obstacles.

Troops can be spawned beside and/or on top of obstacles.

After the Halloween 2014 update, the required time to remove all obstacles except Mushrooms was reduced.

Spawn Mechanics

Vegetation respawns at a rate of one item per eight hours, except if all the spaces in your village are totally filled, or the 45-obstacle limit has been reached.

With the exception of the Gem Box, an obstacle will only spawn if there is a 1-tile buffer zone between the spaces in which it will spawn and another building or obstacle.

Since vegetation respawns and removing one may have Gems, removing vegetation regularly can be an unlimited (although sometimes slow) source of Gems.

Rocks do not respawn once they are removed.

Special event obstacles only appear for a limited time, and are usually created to commemorate holidays. After the period in which they spawn ends, players can continue to keep these obstacles. Note that all special obstacles ignore the one-tile buffer zone rule that governs where normal vegetation can spawn. They can spawn in any 2x2 empty space within your village, though they tend toward spawning close to the outer edges of the field.

Removal Rewards

Each obstacle that is removed can contain anywhere from 0 to 6 Gems.

There are two cycles governing the number of Gems you receive by removing obstacles in your Home Village. These cycles all occur concurrently and are independent of each other, including those for Obstacles in the Builder Base. This means that clearing an obstacle advances only one of the below cycles and has no effect on the position of the other cycles.

The first cycle applies to original obstacles in the Home Village (those that spawned when you first created your Home Village). This cycle has no 4, 5 or 6 gem rewards. The cycle is 3, 0, 1, 2, 0, 1, 1, 0, 0, 3, 1, 0, 2, 2, 0, 0, 3, 0, 1, 0 for initial obstacles.

The second cycle governs the number of Gems you receive from spawned obstacles in your Home Village. It advances when you remove an obstacle in the Home Village. The cycle is 6, 0, 4, 5, 1, 3, 2, 0, 0, 5, 1, 0, 3, 4, 0, 0, 5, 0, 1, 0 for spawned obstacles.

On average, each obstacle cleared has 2 Gems. This pattern does not include the 25 Gems held from the Gem Box.

Gem Cycle

The gem cycles are as detailed in the Removal Rewards section above.

Special Obstacles

During holidays like Halloween, Christmas (Clashmas), and Lunar New Year, special obstacles spawn. These are highly coveted because they have unique designs for each year and ignore the 45-obstacle limit!

Clashmas Trees

Clashmas Trees are a prominent special obstacle, with different versions of them appearing in every winter period since the game's launch in 2012. Each Clashmas tree takes 30s to remove and gives 5 Experience. Clashmas trees were originally named "X-Mas Tree", which was later changed to "Clashmas Tree", and then even later changed to specify the year they originated from.

Ancient Debris (Rocks)

Rocks are a special class of obstacle present only when you first create your village. Unlike trees, Rocks do not respawn.

Once a rock is removed, it is gone forever. This has made them a symbol of prestige for "Permabase" accounts that preserve their original rocks. With the introduction of the Shovel of Obstacles, these ancient stones can now be moved to the edge of the map, allowing chiefs to keep them as trophies without hindering their base layout.
